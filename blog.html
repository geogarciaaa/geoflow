5 Reasons Contractors Lose Leads (And How to Fix Each One)

Most contractors are working harder than ever — but quietly losing 40-60% of their inbound leads without even realizing it. Here's exactly why it happens, and what to do about it.

01Missed calls with no follow-up

This is the big one. You're on a job, your phone rings, you can't answer. The caller gets voicemail. Studies show that 62% of people who get voicemail don't leave a message and don't call back — they just move on to the next contractor on Google.

"I called three roofers. First one to call me back got the job." — This is your potential customer, every single day.
The fix

Auto-text every missed caller within 2 minutes. Something simple: "Hey it's Mike — we're on a job right now but I don't want to leave you hanging. Want a free estimate? Just text back." That one message keeps them warm until you can call back.

02Slow response time

Speed matters more than almost anything else in local service businesses. Research shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes makes you 21x more likely to qualify them compared to responding in 30 minutes. After an hour, most leads have already moved on.

The fix

Automated responses bridge the gap. Even if you can't personally call back for 2 hours, an auto-text in 2 minutes tells the lead "you matter, I'll be in touch." That alone keeps them from calling the next guy.

03No Google reviews (or not enough)

When someone searches "roofer near me," they're comparing you to 5 other companies in 30 seconds. If you have 8 reviews and your competitor has 80, you lose — even if you do better work. Most contractors get a handful of reviews a year simply because nobody asks.

The fix

After every completed job, send your customer a text asking for a Google review. Most happy customers are glad to leave one — they just never think to do it on their own. Automating this one thing can get you 3-5 new reviews every week.

04A bad (or no) website

A lot of contractors are running on a Facebook page from 2017 or a website that looks like it was built on a flip phone. The moment a potential customer lands on a slow, ugly, or confusing site — they leave. First impressions are everything online.

The fix

You don't need a fancy website. You need a clean, fast, mobile-friendly page that answers three questions: What do you do? Where do you do it? How do I contact you? That's it. A $500 website done right beats a $5,000 site done wrong.

05No system for after-hours leads

A lot of people search for contractors in the evening after work — 6pm, 8pm, even midnight. If your website has no way to capture that interest (no chat, no form, no clear CTA), those potential customers just close the tab and forget about you by morning.

The fix

A simple AI chatbot on your website answers basic questions 24/7 and captures contact info. "What areas do you serve? Do you do free estimates? How quickly can you come out?" — if something can answer those questions at 11pm, you've got a lead waiting for you in the morning.
