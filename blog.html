5 Reasons Contractors Lose Leads (And How to Fix Each One)

Most contractors are working harder than ever — but quietly losing 40-60% of their inbound leads without realizing it. Here's why, and what to do about it.

1Missed calls with no follow-up

The big one. You're on a job, the phone rings, you can't answer. 62% of people who hit voicemail don't leave a message and don't call back — they move to the next contractor on Google.

"I called three roofers. First one to call me back got the job." — your potential customer, every single day
The fix

Auto-text every missed caller within 2 minutes. Something simple: "Hey it's Mike — on a job right now but don't want to leave you hanging. Want a free estimate? Just text back."

2Slow response time

Speed beats almost everything in local service. Respond within 5 minutes and you're 21x more likely to qualify the lead than at 30 minutes. After an hour, most have moved on.

21×

more likely to close when you respond in 5 minutes vs. 30.

The fix

Automation bridges the gap. Even if you can't call back for 2 hours, an auto-text in 2 minutes says "you matter, I'll be in touch" — and keeps them from calling the next guy.

3No Google reviews (or not enough)

Someone searching "roofer near me" compares you to 5 companies in 30 seconds. 8 reviews vs. your competitor's 80? You lose — even if your work is better.

The fix

After every completed job, text your customer a review link. Happy customers are glad to leave one — they just never think of it. This alone can add 3-5 reviews a week.

4A bad (or no) website

A slow, confusing, or 2017-Facebook-page web presence loses customers instantly. First impressions are everything online.

The fix

You don't need fancy. You need clean, fast, and mobile-friendly, answering three things: What do you do? Where? How do I contact you? A $500 site done right beats a $5,000 site done wrong.

5No system for after-hours leads

People search for contractors at 6pm, 8pm, midnight. No chat, no form, no clear CTA? They close the tab and forget you by morning.

The fix

A simple chatbot answers basics 24/7 — service area, free estimates, response time — and captures contact info so a lead is waiting for you in the morning.
